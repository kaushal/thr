Micheal Ferrari

On February 14th, 2011, my mother, Karen Ferrari, passed away from a long, two year battle with her sixth brain tumor. I was completely devastated. My mother had an extremely positive outlook on life. It was as if every aspect of life, to her, was intriguing and wonderful, despite how bad things might have become. I had been prepared for this day to come, but it was much different when it actually had occurred. Thrive has been an outlet for me then, and even now in 2013, in a multitude of ways. I wear my Thrive bracelet very often, and whenever I happen to have a thought of sadness or anger about what had occurred in my life, I take a look at my wrist, and see the word "Thrive." What this one, simple little word says to me is, 'Look at your life, and make the best of it. Mommy is watching your every move, and making everything better. She will ALWAYS be in your heart, and she will always love you. Live your life like she did, with the most positive have been through. I keep a positive outlook on life, just like my Mommy did and I live my life to the fullest. People know what I have been through, and the fact that I can live a happy life with my head held high, and make other people happy at the same time, then we're on to something. Thank you, Thrive!
